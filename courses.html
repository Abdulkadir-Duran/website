Board Of Directors

Board of directors (BOD) is those responsible for the overall administration and management of the organizationâ€™s affairs.

Board Elections:

SOPAD members will elect the board among themselves once a year. If a board member resigns, dies, or meets long illness other members of the organization will replace him/her as soon as possible.

Board Chairperson

- Prepares the committee meetings and agenda.
- Serve as the head of the board.
- Act as a spokes person for the board.
- Consult with the board, and evaluate their performance.
- Conducting orientation, if new members are chosen.

Board Vice Chairperson

- Come board meetings each time.
- Serve as the executive chair person.
- Learn more about the responsibilities of the chair person to carry-out his job when the chair person is away.

Board Secretary

- Safely maintain the records and documents of the board.
- Provide board to serve as the ex-person of the vice president by doing his/her job if he/she is absent.
- Get information and notice If a member is absent, and check for the cause.

Board Treasurer

- Have the knowledge of accounting and at that time can perform objectives of the organization.
- Act as a financial officer of the organization and review it periodically.
- Prepare appropriate financial reports of the organization.

Board Auditor

- Evaluate economy and efficiency with which scarce resources are utilized.
- Control policies and procedures established by the board.
- Determine un-established objectives have been achieved.
- Ensure that the financial statements and organizational reports are safe guarded.
- Evaluate the effects of the errors or irregularities uncovered during the process on the financial report.

Meeting of the Board

- SOPAD board members will meet once a month.
- The meeting will happen if 2/3 of the board members attend.
- The chairperson of the board will be the guider of the meeting.

Organizational Core Value

- Child/women participation.
- Child/women survival/development/protection.
- Non-discrimination and equality.
- Respect for child/women rights.
- Transparency.
